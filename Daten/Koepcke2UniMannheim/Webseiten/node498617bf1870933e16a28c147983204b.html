Black HD 280 PRO 1/8in. and 1/4in. (3.5mm and 6.3mm) Connector Circumaural Professional DJ and Studio Headphone


These rugged, comfortable headphones are designed for professional monitoring applications. Features include single-sided coiled cable, rotating ear cups, and up to 32dB noise attenuation.


 

A truly professional set of headphones for the discerning listener. Hear what you want to hear! The HD 280PRO from Sennheiser produces thrilling true-to-life sound, while keeping outside sound where it should be: outside. Compatible with 3.5mm connectors, these headphones fit around the ear. These are collapsible, and interfold to fit into small spaces. Neodymium magnets are built in for maximum SPL.

Resounding bass and twinkling soprano combine for beautiful sound with these headphones. Comfortable, with their fitted earcups for 32dB of ambient noise attenuation. Powerful, with their accuracy of sound. And of course, durable, for professionals who don't want to waste money!

Thrill to the thundering bass as Wagner's "Ride of the Valkyries" throbs from your stereo, or mellow out to the glorious alto of Mama Cass Elliot, while these comfortable headphones cancel out the nightly battles of your next door neighbors. Hear music as it should be heard, whether in the privacy of your own home or when you're running the sound and light board at a concert! Comfortable, professional and durable. Hear your music on a professional level. Order the Sennheiser HD 280 Pro, today!

 
 
  • Dynamic, closed-ear headphones with up to 32dB attenuation of outside sound
  • Lightweight and comfortable, ergonomic design, Cord Length: 3.3ft - 9.8ft coiled

Rating: 5/5Great Sound, but not the best for use when out and about.

This review is from: Sennheiser HD280 Pro Around the Ear DJ Headphones

Pros: Great sound
Good padding
Long cord
Noise isolation

Cons: Ears cups may get warm
A bit heavy
Fall off if you move your head a lot

Other Thoughts: Overall these are great sounding headphones. I got them on sale for $75. Well worth it.The Bass sounds great without drowning out Mids and Highs like some overpriced headphones (Talking about you Beats). Well balanced sound, not bass heavy. I cant wait to use these with a DAC.

My cons about these headphones is that they are just a bit to large and the ear cups get warm. The size of the headband and the clamp of is causes them to be comfortable when wearing on a desk, but causes them to fall off if trying to wear around town. Yes you prob don't want wear these to head bang, but if you plan to play drums or move around a lot with these on you will find yourself having to place these back on your ears often. The other con that when I wear these on after a long period of time, the ear cups get warm. This just might be a personal thing so room temp and head type my affect this.

Someone mentioned that the stitching on the ear cups was poor and had torn on them. I have had these for 5 years now and the only wear and tear issue I have is with the pad on the top of the headband. This pad and the ear-pads can be replaced but may be a pain to put back on.
