    A.US Carriers work on different 4G LTE bands, so this unlocked LG G4 H815 32GB GSM 4G LTE Hexa-Core Android Smartphone will only work on a carrier whose 4G LTE supports the same band that this phone works on. The supported 4G (LTE ) Bands on this phone are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 17, 20, 28. Cricket mobile and AT & T use bands 2 (1900 MHz), 4 (1700 MHZ a, b, c, d and e) and 17 (700 MHz b, c) and T-Mobile uses bands 2 (1900 Mhz), 4 (1700 MHz d,e,f). and 12 (700 MHz a). Most of the other carriers use bands 4 and 17. Verizon uses band 2 (1900 MHz), 4 (1700 MHz, f), 13 (700 MHz, c) Sprint uses band 2 (1900 MHz), band 25 (1900 MHz, g), 26 ( 850 MHz). The 4G LTE band that all these carriers have in common appears to be band 2 (1900 MHz), but you would be best advised to contact them to inquire about the phones compatibility with their services...^IFV

    The charger that came with my phone won't work with this phone or any other phone that I know of. The connector is made for some other completely different type of device. When I first opened the package and noticed it I thought maybe this was what you got and you were expected to buy an additional adapter of some sort. I discussed it with LG two times by chat site and also by phone conversation. They all indicated that I should have gotten the right charger with the phone and that I should return it to Walmart. I also discussed it with people in the Electronics departments at several other Walmart stores. I seriously considered keeping the phone and just buying a charger for it. But, I thought with a mistake like this, maybe their could be another problem-like it being the wrong version(International instead of US?). I returned it to Walmart for a complete refund. To Walmart's credit-they made the process very hassle free. What I discovered was inside the factory sealed box so it is an LG mistake not a Walmart mistake. I have been out in the rain for so long with Walmart online trying to order this particular phone that I am not absolutely sure if I'll repurchase this phone or something else and whatever I get-where I will purchase it. I really think this is what I want but I just can't believe I would experience this kind of problem with a phone costing over $400
